Here is the time line of Mandela's life:

  • July 18 1918: Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela is born in Mvezo, Umtata, in the then Cape Province of South Africa.

  • 1941: Mandela moves to Johannesburg, where he begins his studies and works as a mine security officer.
  • July 26 1944: Mandela, Walter Sisulu, Oliver Tambo, and others form the African National Congress (ANC) Youth League.
  • 1948: The National Party wins elections, establishing apartheid, a system of institutionalized racial segregation and discrimination.
  • 1952: Mandela becomes the National Volunteer-in-Chief of the ANC's Defiance Campaign against unjust laws.
  • 1955: The ANC adopts the Freedom Charter at the Congress of the People in Kliptown, which articulates the vision for a democratic South Africa.
  • 1956: Mandela is arrested and charged with high treason, along with 155 others. They are eventually acquitted in 1961.
  • 1961: The ANC's military wing, Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK), is established, with Mandela as one of its leaders. It marks the start of armed resistance against apartheid.
  • 1962: Mandela is arrested and sentenced to five years in prison for leaving the country illegally and inciting strikes.
  • 1978: The international movement for Mandela's release gains momentum, including the famous "Free Nelson Mandela" campaign.
  • February 2 1989: South African President F.W. de Klerk announces the beginning of negotiations to end apartheid.
  • February 11 1990: Mandela is released from prison after 27 years.
  • 1981: The ANC and the South African government begin formal negotiations to end apartheid at the Convention for a Democratic South Africa (CODESA).
  • 1993: Mandela and de Klerk are awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to dismantle apartheid and establish a multi-racial democracy
  • April 27 1994: South Africa holds its first democratic elections, and Nelson Mandela is elected President, marking the end of apartheid
  • 1999: Mandela steps down as President, succeeded by Thabo Mbeki, and begins his retirement from political life.
  • 2001: Mandela publicly announces that his son, Makgatho Mandela, has died of AIDS-related complications, which helps raise awareness about HIV/AIDS in South Africa.
  • 2004: Mandela officially retires from public life, though he continues to be a prominent figure in various humanitarian efforts.
  • December 5 2013: Nelson Mandela dies at the age of 95 in Johannesburg. His death is met with a global outpouring of grief and tributes.

Nelson Mandela's legacy is marked by his steadfast commitment to justice, equality, and reconciliation
